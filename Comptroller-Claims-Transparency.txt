A LOCAL LAW

To amend the New York city charter, in relation to adding violations to the interactive crime map.

Be it enacted by the Council as follows:
Section 1. Subdivision s of section 1072 of the New York city charter is amended to read as follows:
s. to provide to the public, at no charge on the city's website, an interactive claims map that[, for each segment of a street bounded by one or more intersections and/or a terminus,] shall visually display, and provide in a machine readable format (ALL THIS DATA MUST BE AVAILABLE VIA SIMPLE RESTful API), the aggregate monthly, yearly and year-to-date totals for the current and the most recent prior calendar years for every claim submitted, as well as each amount of claim and settlement offer or rejection and starting date, and settlement offer date and whether the settlement is accepted by the claimant. Such map shall display the location of each claim with as much specificity as possible, including latitude and longitude if possible, but in all cases at least as specific as the nearest intersection, as well as date and time information. Such map shall be searchable by address, zip code, and causing agency (e.g., FDNY, NYPD, etc).  All information required by this subdivision shall be available on the city's website as soon as [practicable] possible but in no case more than one month after a claim has been submitted. The mayor shall ensure that all agencies provide the department with such assistance and information as the department requires to compile and update the interactive claim map.
ยง2.  This local law shall take effect sixty days after its enactment into law.
